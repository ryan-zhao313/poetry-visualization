What Does It Mean To Be Perfect

by Ryan Zhao

  • In the mythic guise of a "model" dream,
  • the whispers of perfection softly gleam.
  • They spoke of a myth, a dual-edged sword,
  • a stereotype born, yet widely ignored.
  • Most are given only four paths to follow,
  • become a doctor, lawyer, scientist, or engineer tomorrow.
  • To follow paths predefined, a set array,
  • Yet dreams are diverse, in vibrant disarray.
  • We are supposed to excel in American schools,
  • yet we are treated like outsiders, used as tools.
  • They tell us we all look the same.
  • So tell me why is there disparity? Who's to blame?
  • The myth erases, it simplifies, it denies,
  • the nuances, struggles, the lows, the highs.
  • Expectations trail us wherever we roam,
  • From school to work, they shape our home.
  • What does it mean to be perfect, we ask?
  • I'd say it's the hard work and struggles, our daily task.
  • They claim perfection, solely by our eyes,
  • But perfect doesn't exist, it's all built on deceitful lies.
